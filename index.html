Skills for the midcentury

This initiative started as a father's advice for his daughter, who will be 20 in 2031.

Think about ages: she will be 39 in 2050; her children will live into the next century.

The world she is experiencing is not the same as the one I grew up in during the 1900s. Yet, higher education hasn't changed much since the last century. A college education has never been enough for anyone. We spend nearly 50 years of our lives employed after college.

These lessons for the middle of the 21st century are for those who never stop improving.
